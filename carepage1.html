Plant Care Tips

Monstera

When potting your Monstera deliciosa, plant in well-draining soil in a pot with drainage holes to prevent it getting wet feet.
Top tip: monstera deliciosa is a climber in its natural habitat, using its aerial roots to cling to large trees, so you should provide it with some kind of stake or a trellis to support it.

Snake Plant

Grow the snake plant in good light, but away from direct sunlight, to keep the leaf colours bright and beautiful.
These unusual-looking house plants thrive best in gritty, free-draining compost, which should be allowed to dry out completely between waterings.

Ferns

Happiest in a cooler, shady spot like a bathroom or kitchen.
Maidenhair, asparagus and sword ferns are particularly easy to grow.
Keep the compost moist at all times and mist the foliage regularly with a hand sprayer.

Spider Plants

These boys prefer a cool, light position away from direct sunlight, otherwise their foliage will fade and weaken.
Water regularly during the Summer, but allow the compost to dry out completely between waterings in the Winter.

Potted Ivy

Plant ivy in an all-purpose potting soil, in a pot with drainage.
Let the top of the soil dry to the touch between waterings, and fertilize your ivy about once a month in the spring, summer, and fall.
Especially in dry, winter air, it will benefit from regular misting of the foliage.

Yucca Tree

Yuccas need good light, but no direct sunlight as it can damage the leaves.
These houseplants can be top-heavy, so ensure they’re planted in a soil-based compost so that they don’t tip over when dry!
They only need occasional watering – pictured here alongside a healthy monstera!
