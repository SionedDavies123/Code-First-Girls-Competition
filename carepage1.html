Plant Care Tips

Aloe Vera

Aloe vera should be grown out of direct sunlight but in a bright position, ideally the kitchen.
Keep soil damp but not wet, and cut down on watering in Winter.
Top tip: for burns, cuts and scratches – break a bit of a leaf off and squeeze out the healing sap!

Ferns

Happiest in a cooler, shady spot like a bathroom or kitchen.
Maidenhair, asparagus and sword ferns are particularly easy to grow.
Keep the compost moist at all times and mist the foliage regularly with a hand sprayer.

Cacti

Happiest in a really sunny spot!
They need a gritty and free-draining soil (ask in the garden centre if you’re not sure) and only very occasional watering.
Top tip: overwatering is the main source of cacti death – be careful to avoid!

Spider Plants

These boys prefer a cool, light position away from direct sunlight, otherwise their foliage will fade and weaken.
Water regularly during the Summer, but allow the compost to dry out completely between waterings in the Winter.

Potted Ivy

Plant ivy in an all-purpose potting soil, in a pot with drainage.
Let the top of the soil dry to the touch between waterings, and fertilize your ivy about once a month in the spring, summer, and fall.
Especially in dry, winter air, it will benefit from regular misting of the foliage.

Peace Lily

Peace lilies need good light (but NOT direct sunlight) to encourage the white flowers to bloom.
Overwatering is also a common demise for the peace lily – only water them when the compost is dry to the touch!
